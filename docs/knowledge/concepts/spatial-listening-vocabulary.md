# Spatial Listening Vocabulary

Type: concept note
Source IDs:

- `spatial-hearing-revised-edition`
- `principles-and-applications-of-spatial-hearing`

## Purpose

This note turns the spatial-hearing seed sources into Axiom listening-record
vocabulary. It does not claim that Axiom creates, improves, or preserves any
specific spatial cue by itself.

## Axiom Test-Design Use

Use these terms when a listening target involves width, bass-image attachment,
headphone presentation, or center-image stability:

- `center_image`: whether centered anchors such as vocal, kick, snare, and bass
  stay stable;
- `lateral_spread`: whether side energy feels wider, narrower, or exaggerated;
- `localization_blur`: whether sources become harder to place;
- `depth_impression`: whether the scene feels flatter or more layered;
- `bass_image_coupling`: whether bass weight remains attached to the kick,
  center image, or main musical object;
- `route_context`: whether the observation came from speakers, headphones,
  crossfeed, Bluetooth, wired, Android, or WSL/JDSP.

## Axiom Questions

- Does a width or bass change move the apparent center, or only change tonal
  weight?
- Does elevated Sub Harmonics make bass feel detached from the main image?
- Does headphone listening reveal localization blur that speaker listening does
  not?
- Are changes route-specific enough to belong in a profile or host setting
  instead of Axiom Core?

## Boundary

Spatial vocabulary can make listening notes more precise. It is not listening
acceptance and is not proof of a spatial improvement.
